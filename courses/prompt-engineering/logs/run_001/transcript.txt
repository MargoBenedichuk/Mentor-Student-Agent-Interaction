Here's the core idea: a prompt works best when it gives the model exactly **one action** to take, expressed with exactly **one imperative verb**, right up front. When you cram multiple verbs into one sentence — "summarize this and translate it and also check the tone" — the model tends to latch onto one and quietly skip the rest. The fix is simple: identify the single verb, put it first, and move every constraint or formatting note *after* it. If you genuinely need two actions, split them into two separate prompts or a numbered list — never a single run-on.

So, in your own words: **what does the 1-task-1-verb rule say, and why does mixing two verbs in one instruction usually go wrong?**
