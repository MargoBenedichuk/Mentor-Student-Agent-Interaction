**Lesson 1: Clear Instruction**

The core discipline is the **1-task-1-verb rule**: a well-formed prompt states exactly one action, expressed with exactly one imperative verb, before any constraints or format specs. When you cram multiple verbs into one sentence — "summarize this and translate it and check the tone" — the model tends to latch onto the first or most prominent action and silently drop the rest. If a task genuinely needs two actions, split them into separate prompts or clearly ordered steps; treat the word "and" between two top-level verbs as your signal to break things apart.
