Here's the core idea: **the 1-task-1-verb rule.** A well-formed prompt states exactly one action using exactly one imperative verb, and it does that *before* any constraints or formatting notes. When you cram two verbs into one sentence — "summarize this and also translate it and keep the tone friendly" — the model tends to latch onto one and silently drop the other. The word "and" between two top-level verbs is your signal to split the prompt in two.
